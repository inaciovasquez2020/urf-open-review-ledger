Invariant Index
Invariant 1: Capacity Bound
Definition:
C is the maximal information (bits) transmissible or refinable per step under admissibility constraints.
Parameterization:
C = C(system, locality radius R, transcript budget)
Primary Obstruction:
Unbounded refinement depth is impossible under finite C.
Reduces the following results to this invariant:
Chronos
EntropyDepth lower bounds
Transcript Capacity Theorem
Invariant 2: Locality Radius
Definition:
R bounds the radius of neighborhoods accessible to definable or operational probes.
Parameterization:
R = R(k, Δ) for FO^k on bounded-degree graphs
Primary Obstruction:
Global distinction cannot be certified by purely local probes.
Reduces the following results to this invariant:
FO^k locality collapse
EF-game saturation
Cycle-local rigidity
Invariant 3: Spectral Gap
Definition:
λ₁ > 0 is a uniform lower bound on the nonzero spectrum of the governing operator.
Parameterization:
λ₁ = λ₁(system)
Primary Obstruction:
Long-range degrees of freedom are viotropic (operationally null).
Reduces the following results to this invariant:
Spectral Gap Rigidity
Yang–Mills reduction
Dimensional escape closure
Invariant 4: Admissibility
Definition:
Only operations respecting alignment, sensing, and capacity constraints are allowed.
Parameterization:
Admissible class A
Primary Obstruction:
Non-admissible refinement paths are excluded from witness construction.
Reduces the following results to this invariant:
Admissibility Axioms
Normalization theorems
No-free-refinement corollaries
Invariant 5: Rigidity
Definition:
Local indistinguishability forces global equivalence under bounded capacity.
Parameterization:
(R, C, λ₁)
Primary Obstruction:
Persistent ambiguity implies structural collapse or explosion.
Reduces the following results to this invariant:
Unified Rigidity Framework
Local–Global Rigidity Transfer
Terminal wall statements
