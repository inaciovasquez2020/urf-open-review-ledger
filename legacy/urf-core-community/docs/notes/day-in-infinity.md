---
title: A Day in Infinity
status: explanatory
scope: intuition / normalization
---

In an infinite setting, there is no natural clock.

A “day” does not have a fixed length the way 24 hours does in ordinary life. Any amount of time you choose can be rescaled without changing anything essential, because infinity provides no reference point that makes one duration special.

Saying “a day in infinity” therefore means choosing a unit purely for convenience. It is a way to label progress or organize thought, not a statement about an absolute amount of time.

In the URF perspective, this mirrors capacity:
- with finite capacity, time steps have meaning because progress per step is bounded;
- with infinite capacity, all finite durations collapse into the same class.

So a day in infinity is not a length of time.  
It is a normalization choice.
