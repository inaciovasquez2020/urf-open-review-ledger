\begin{lemma}[Flip preserves horizontality]
If $T \subset J^k(X)$ supports a horizontal normal function, then
$\mathsf{Flip}(T)$ also supports a horizontal normal function.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Horizontality is expressed by the condition
\[
\nabla(T) \subset F^{k-1} H^{2k-1}(X,\mathbb{C}),
\]
where $\nabla$ is the Gauss--Manin connection.
Since $\nabla$ is $\mathbb{Q}$-linear, it preserves the rational envelope of $T$,
hence preserves $\mathsf{Flip}(T)$.
\end{proof}
