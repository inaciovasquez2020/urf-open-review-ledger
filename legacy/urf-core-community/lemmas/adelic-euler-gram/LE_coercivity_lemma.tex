\begin{lemma}[Néron--Tate coercivity on the real Mordell--Weil space]
Let $E/\mathbb{Q}$ be an elliptic curve and let
$V:=E(\mathbb{Q})\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}}\mathbb{R}$.
The Néron--Tate pairing $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_{\mathrm{NT}}$ extends to a
positive definite bilinear form on $V$.
Hence for any fixed norm $\|\cdot\|$ on $V$ there exists a constant $c(E,\|\cdot\|)>0$
such that
\[
\langle v,v\rangle_{\mathrm{NT}} \ge c(E,\|\cdot\|)\,\|v\|^2
\qquad \forall v\in V.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The restriction of $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_{\mathrm{NT}}$ to
$E(\mathbb{Q})/E(\mathbb{Q})_{\mathrm{tors}}$ is a positive definite quadratic form.
Tensoring with $\mathbb{R}$ yields a finite-dimensional real vector space $V$
equipped with a positive definite bilinear form.
On a finite-dimensional real vector space, any two norms are equivalent and every
positive definite quadratic form is coercive with respect to any fixed norm.
\end{proof}
