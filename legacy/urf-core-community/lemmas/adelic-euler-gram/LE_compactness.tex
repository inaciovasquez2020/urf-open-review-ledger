\begin{lemma}[Compactness of $\mathcal L_E$]
The Euler--Gram operator $\mathcal L_E$ is compact, self-adjoint, and positive
on $\ell^2(E(\mathbb{Q}))$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The kernel $K_E(P,Q)$ is symmetric and satisfies
\[
\sum_{P,Q \in E(\mathbb{Q})} |K_E(P,Q)|^2 < \infty,
\]
since the Néron--Tate height is quadratic and the real Mordell--Weil space is
finite dimensional. Hence $\mathcal L_E$ is a Hilbert--Schmidt operator.
Every Hilbert--Schmidt operator is compact and self-adjoint.
\end{proof}
