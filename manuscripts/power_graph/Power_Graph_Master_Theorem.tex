\begin{theorem}[Entropy Depth Lower Bound via Power Graph]
Let $G_n$ be a graph on $n$ vertices.
Let $T_n$ denote the minimal number of sequential $R$-local refinement steps
needed to activate all vertices.

Then
\[
T_n \ge \chi(G_n^{(2R)}).
\]
In particular,
\[
T_n \ge \frac{n}{\alpha(G_n^{(2R)})}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Vertices at distance $\le 2R$ cannot activate simultaneously.
Each refinement layer forms an independent set in $G_n^{(2R)}$.
Thus the number of layers is at least the chromatic number.
The second inequality follows from $\chi \ge n/\alpha$.
\end{proof}
