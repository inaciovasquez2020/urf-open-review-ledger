\section{Integration with Entropy Depth Framework}

Let $T$ denote refinement depth under $R$-local activation.

We have:

\[
T \ge \chi(G_n^{(2R)}).
\]

Combined with structural results:

\begin{itemize}
\item If $\Delta=O(1)$ and $R=O(1)$:
\[
T = O(1).
\]

\item If $R=\Theta(\log n)$ in small-diameter families:
\[
T = \Theta(n).
\]

\item If $\operatorname{diam}(G_n) \le 2R$:
\[
T = n.
\]
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Structural Principle.}
Entropy depth is controlled not by expansion alone,
but by metric collapse of $2R$-neighborhood geometry.
