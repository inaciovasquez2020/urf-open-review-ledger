\begin{theorem}[Mixed Hodge Horizontal Rigidity]
Let $T \subset J^k(X)$ be a real subtorus supporting a horizontal normal function,
possibly in a mixed Hodge variation.
Then
\[
T = \mathsf{Flip}(T),
\]
and hence $T$ is defined over $\mathbb{Q}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the previous lemma, $\mathsf{Flip}(T)$ is horizontal.
By Deligne's theory of absolute Hodge classes, any horizontal rational
sub-Hodge structure is algebraic.
Therefore $T$ must already equal its rational closure.
\end{proof}
